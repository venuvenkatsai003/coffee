Different various of Brewed Coffee

Cappuccino

Beyond Italy, cappuccino is a coffee drink that today is typically composed of a single, double, or triple espresso shot and hot milk, with the surface topped with foamed milk. Cappuccinos are most often prepared with an espresso machine.

Marocchino

Quite simply the marocchino is a shot of espresso with a layer of chocolate-powder-dusted foamy milk on top, and served in a glass. More often than not, the barista starts with hot chocolate or a dusting of cocoa powder at the bottom of the glass before pouring the espresso.

A cafetière, also known as a French press, is a coffee brewing device that separates coffee grounds from the brewed coffee. Cafetières are heatproof jugs, usually made of glass, with a strainer attached to the lid. After brewing, the strainer is plunged to the bottom of the cafetière to separate the coffee from the grounds. The coffee can then be poured from the spout, while the grounds remain in the base.

Espresso

Put simply, an espresso is a type of strong black coffee that's created when hot water is forced through ground coffee beans. While this may sound similar to a standard cup of coffee, the two are very different. Espresso requires significantly more pressure and a faster brewing time than coffee.
